<p><p>Notebook batteries can be broadly categorized into two types: rechargeable batteries and non-rechargeable batteries. Rechargeable batteries, also known as secondary batteries, can be reused by recharging them after they have been drained of power. These batteries are commonly found in most modern notebooks and are preferred for their long-term cost-effectiveness and convenience. On the other hand, non-rechargeable batteries, also known as primary batteries, cannot be recharged and must be replaced once they are depleted. While non-rechargeable batteries tend to have a shorter lifespan and higher cost over time, they may be suitable for situations where regular access to charging is limited or unavailable.</p></p>
