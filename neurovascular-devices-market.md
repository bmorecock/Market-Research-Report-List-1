<p><p>Neurovascular devices are medical devices that are used to treat neurovascular disorders, which primarily include conditions affecting the blood vessels of the brain and spinal cord. These devices are designed to restore blood flow, remove clots, or provide structural support to the affected blood vessels.</p><p>The neurovascular devices market has witnessed significant growth in recent years, primarily due to the increasing prevalence of neurovascular disorders such as ischemic strokes and cerebral aneurysms. Additionally, the rising geriatric population, growing healthcare expenditure, and advancements in technology have also contributed to the market growth.</p><p>According to market research, the neurovascular devices market is projected to grow at a CAGR of 6.5% during the forecast period. The market is being driven by the increasing adoption of minimally invasive procedures, improving healthcare infrastructure, and the development of innovative neurovascular devices. These devices offer several advantages, such as reduced recovery time, lower risk of complications, and better patient outcomes.</p><p>Some of the latest trends observed in the neurovascular devices market include the introduction of advanced imaging technologies, the development of bioresorbable stents, and the integration of robotics in neurovascular surgeries. Advanced imaging technologies, such as 3D rotational angiography and high-resolution MRI, enable accurate diagnosis and treatment planning. Bioresorbable stents eliminate the need for long-term antiplatelet therapy and prevent long-term vessel damage. The integration of robotics in neurovascular surgeries allows for precise and controlled interventions, improving patient safety and outcomes.</p><p>In summary, the neurovascular devices market is experiencing steady growth, driven by the increasing prevalence of neurovascular disorders and advancements in technology.
