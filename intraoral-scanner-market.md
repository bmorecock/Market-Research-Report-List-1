<p><p>An intraoral scanner is a dental device that captures digitally accurate three-dimensional (3D) images of the teeth and surrounding oral tissues. It replaces the need for traditional physical impressions, enhancing the accuracy, efficiency, and comfort of dental procedures. The scanner uses laser or optical technology to capture images, which are then processed and used for various dental applications, including restorative dentistry, orthodontics, and prosthetics.</p><p>The global intraoral scanner market is projected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 12.6% during the forecast period. Several factors contribute to this growth. Firstly, there is an increasing demand for advanced dental technology and digital solutions in dental practices, driven by the need for improved patient comfort and efficient workflows. Intraoral scanners provide faster and more accurate results compared to traditional methods, leading to enhanced patient satisfaction and reduced chairside time.</p><p>The market growth is also influenced by the rising prevalence of dental disorders and the growing geriatric population worldwide.
