Unlike traditional brushed DC motors that have a core, coreless motors do not have an iron core in the rotor. <p><p>The coreless brushed DC motor market consists of two main types: cylindrical motors and disc motors. Cylindrical motors feature a cylindrical shape with a rotor that rotates inside the stator. They are commonly used in applications where high power and compact size are required. On the other hand, disc motors have a disc-shaped rotor that spins between two stator plates. They are ideal for applications that require high torque and precise control. Both types offer advantages in terms of size, power, and performance, catering to different requirements in the market.</p></p>
