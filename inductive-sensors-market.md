<p><strong>The Inductive Sensors Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Self Inductive Type</li><li>Mutual Inductive Type</li><li>Eddy Current Type</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Inductive sensors are electronic devices used for detecting the presence, position, or movement of nearby objects. They work based on the principles of magnetic fields and electromagnetic induction. There are three types of inductive sensors commonly used in the market. </p><p>The Self Inductive Type uses an oscillator coil to create a magnetic field and detects changes in the field caused by the presence of a metallic object.</p><p>The Mutual Inductive Type consists of two coils, one for generating the magnetic field and the other for sensing the changes in the field caused by the object.</p><p>The Eddy Current Type generates an alternating magnetic field that induces eddy currents in a conductive target, and the changes in the induced currents are used to detect the presence of the object.</p></p>
