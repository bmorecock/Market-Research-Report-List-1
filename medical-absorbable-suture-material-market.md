<p><p>The medical absorbable suture material market consists of two types: synthetic material and natural material. Synthetic materials are man-made substances that can be absorbed by the body over time. They are typically made from polymers such as polyglycolic acid or polydioxanone. On the other hand, natural materials are derived from living organisms and are absorbable by the body's natural processes. These can include materials like catgut, which is made from animal intestines. Both types of materials provide options for surgeons to select the most suitable suture material based on the specific requirements of the patient and surgical procedure.</p></p>
