<p><p>The scientific camera market for fluorescence microscopy consists of various types of cameras, including CMOS cameras, CCD cameras, EMCCD cameras, and other types of cameras. CMOS cameras utilize a complementary metal-oxide-semiconductor sensor, offering lower costs and power consumption, but sacrificing sensitivity and image quality compared to other options. CCD cameras, on the other hand, use a charge-coupled device sensor that provides better sensitivity and image quality but at a higher cost. EMCCD cameras combine the benefits of CCD and electron multiplication technologies, offering higher sensitivity for capturing low-light images. Other camera types may include sCMOS or InGaAs cameras with specific advantages, targeted at certain applications.</p></p>
