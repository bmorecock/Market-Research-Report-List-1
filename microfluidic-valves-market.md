<p><strong>The Microfluidic Valves Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Bubble-based Valves</li><li>Capillary Burst Valves</li><li>PDMS-Based Pneumatic Valve</li><li>Electrostatic Valve</li><li>Time-Delayed Valve</li><li>Electrowetting Valve</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The microfluidic valves market is categorized into various types. Bubble-based valves work by forming bubbles to control the fluid flow. Capillary burst valves rely on a capillary force to disrupt fluid flow. PDMS-Based Pneumatic valves use pressurized air to control the flow. Electrostatic valves operate based on the electrostatic force between electrodes. Time-Delayed valves regulate flow by delaying the opening or closing of the valve. Electrowetting valves adjust flow by manipulating the contact angle of a liquid on a solid surface. Lastly, there are other types of valves available in the market offering different mechanisms to control fluid flow in microfluidic devices.</p></p>
