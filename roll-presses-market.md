The hot rolling market involves heating the material and passing it through a series of rolls to achieve the desired shape or thickness. This process is commonly used in industries like steel production. On the other hand, in the cold rolling market, the material is processed at room temperature, achieving precise sizes and finishes. This method is often used for materials that are sensitive to high temperatures, like aluminum or copper sheets.</p></p>
