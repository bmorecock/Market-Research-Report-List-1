<p><p>Ultrafast rectifiers are electronic components used to convert an alternating current (AC) into a direct current (DC) in high-frequency applications. The market for these rectifiers can be categorized into three types: dual common anode, dual common cathode, and single. In dual common anode rectifiers, two diodes share a common anode terminal, while in dual common cathode rectifiers, two diodes share a common cathode terminal. On the other hand, single rectifiers consist of a single diode, either anode or cathode. These different configurations provide options for designing diverse AC to DC converter circuits based on specific application requirements.</p></p>
