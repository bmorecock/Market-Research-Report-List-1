<p><p>The retail POS terminals market can be divided into three main types. Fixed retail POS terminals are stationary systems that are typically found at traditional checkout counters in stores. They are usually connected to a computer or server and come with a built-in display, cash drawer, and barcode scanner. Mobile retail POS terminals, on the other hand, are portable devices that allow store associates to process transactions anywhere in the store. These terminals are often handheld or tablet-based and provide flexibility for line-busting or in-store customer service. Lastly, the "Others" category encompasses unique or specialized retail POS terminals that may cater to specific industries or functionalities, such as self-service kiosks or integrated payment solutions.</p></p>
