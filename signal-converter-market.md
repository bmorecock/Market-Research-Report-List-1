<p><p>Signal converters are devices that convert signals from one form to another, such as from analog to digital or digital to analog. Analog-to-digital converters (ADC) are used to convert continuous analog signals into discrete digital values, enabling digital processing and storage. Conversely, digital-to-analog converters (DAC) convert digital signals into analog signals to generate continuous waveforms or control analog systems. Both types of converters play crucial roles in various industries, such as telecommunications, audio and video equipment, and industrial automation, enabling efficient signal processing and enhanced performance.</p></p>
