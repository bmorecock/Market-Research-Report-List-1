<p><p>LVDT (Linear Variable Differential Transformer) sensors are position sensors that are used to measure linear displacement. They consist of a primary winding and two secondary windings, each wound on a hollow cylindrical bobbin. As the core of the sensor moves, the voltages induced in the secondary windings change, providing an output signal proportional to the displacement.</p><p>The market for LVDT sensors is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. <p><strong>The LVDT Sensors Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>AC LVDTs</li><li>DC LVDTs</li><li>Spring Loaded LVDTs / Gageheads</li></ul></p>
<p><p>LVDT (Linear Variable Differential Transformer) sensors are commonly used for measuring linear displacement or position. AC LVDTs operate on the principle of mutual induction and are known for their high accuracy and linearity. DC LVDTs use a DC power supply and are often used in applications where AC power is not available. Spring-loaded LVDTs, also known as gageheads, incorporate a spring-loaded mechanism to maintain contact with the measured surface and are widely used in metrology and precision measurement applications. Overall, LVDT sensors offer different types catered to various needs and industries.</p></p>
