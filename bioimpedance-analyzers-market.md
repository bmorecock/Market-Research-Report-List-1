<p><p>Bioimpedance analyzers are devices used to measure the impedance of biological tissues in order to determine information about a person's health. There are two main types of bioimpedance analyzers: wired and wireless. Wired bioimpedance analyzers require a physical connection between the device and the person being measured, typically through electrodes. In contrast, wireless bioimpedance analyzers use wireless technology, such as Bluetooth or Wi-Fi, to transmit data without the need for physical connections. Both types of analyzers provide valuable insights into body composition, hydration levels, and overall health.</p></p>

<p><ul><li>Whole-body Measurement</li><li>Segmental Body Measurement</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Bioimpedance analyzers are devices used to measure the electrical impedance of the body to estimate body composition. Whole-body measurement refers to analyzing the impedance across the entire body, providing insights into overall fat, muscle, and fluid levels. On the other hand, segmental body measurement involves analyzing specific body segments, such as arms, legs, or trunk, to assess their individual composition. Both applications find their use in fitness centers, hospitals, and research institutions to monitor and evaluate the effectiveness of exercise programs, nutritional plans, and health interventions.</p></p>
