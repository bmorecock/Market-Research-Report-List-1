<p><p>Plastic alternative packaging refers to various types of packaging materials that are used as sustainable alternatives to traditional plastic. Starch-based plastics are derived from renewable sources such as corn or potatoes and can decompose easily. Cellulose-based plastics are made from plant fibers such as wood and have good transparency and oxygen barrier properties. Polylactic Acid (PLA) is a biodegradable and compostable material produced from fermented plant sugars. Polyhydroxyalkanoates (PHA) are biodegradable polymers produced by bacteria and can be used to make packaging films. Other types include materials like hemp, mushroom-based packaging, or bio-based plastics made from renewable resources.</p></p>
