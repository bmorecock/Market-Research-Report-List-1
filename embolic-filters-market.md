<p><p>Embolic filters are medical devices used to prevent blood clots from traveling to vital organs such as the heart or lungs during procedures like angioplasty or stent placement. There are two main types of embolic filters: the Upper Chamber Embolic Filter and the Inferior Chamber Embolic Filter. The Upper Chamber Embolic Filter is designed to block blood clots in the upper chambers of the heart, while the Inferior Chamber Embolic Filter is used to capture blood clots in the lower chambers of the heart. Both types of filters are crucial in reducing the risk of complications during cardiovascular procedures.</p></p>
