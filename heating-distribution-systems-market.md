<p><p>Heating distribution systems refer to the various methods used to distribute heat within a building or space. The market types involved in these systems include radiators, furnaces, boilers, heat pumps, active solar heating, electric heating, and others. Radiators are wall-mounted units that emit heat through convection. Furnaces use fuel or electricity to generate warm air that is distributed through ducts. Boilers heat water which is then circulated through pipes to provide central heating. Heat pumps extract heat from the air or ground and transfer it indoors. Active solar heating utilizes solar panels to generate heat. Electric heating systems use electric resistance to produce heat. Other methods encompass various alternative options available in the market.</p></p>
