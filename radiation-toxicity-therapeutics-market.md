<p><p>Radiation toxicity therapeutics market focuses on treating the harmful effects of radiation exposure. Ionizing radiation refers to the high-energy radiation with sufficient power to remove tightly bound electrons from atoms. This type of radiation therapy utilizes devices like X-rays and gamma rays for cancer treatment. Non-ionizing radiation, on the other hand, lacks enough energy to remove electrons. Examples include radio waves and ultraviolet radiation. This type is typically used for various medical imaging techniques, such as MRI and ultrasound. Both ionizing and non-ionizing radiation have distinct applications in the field of radiation toxicity therapeutics.</p></p>
