<p><p>Intraoperative neuromonitoring (IONM) is a technique used during surgical procedures to monitor the integrity and function of the nervous system, such as the brain, spinal cord, and peripheral nerves. This helps surgeons identify and prevent potential nerve damage during the operation.</p><p>The market for intraoperative neuromonitoring is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. The increasing number of surgical procedures coupled with the growing awareness about the benefits of IONM in reducing the risk of nerve damage and improving patient outcomes are driving the market growth.
