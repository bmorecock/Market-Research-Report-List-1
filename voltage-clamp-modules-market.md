<p><p>Voltage clamp modules are devices used in electrophysiology experiments to control and monitor the voltage across a cell membrane. The single electrode voltage clamp module is designed with only one electrode, which both generates the controlled voltage and measures the resulting current. In contrast, the two electrode voltage clamp module employs separate electrodes for voltage control and current measurement, enabling more precise and versatile experiments. Both types serve to accurately study the ion channels and electric properties of cells, aiding in various physiological and pharmacological research.</p></p>
