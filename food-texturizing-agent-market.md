<p><p>The food texturizing agent market can be categorized into five types based on their sources. Plant-derived agents are derived from plants and are commonly used as thickeners or gelling agents. Microbial-derived agents are produced through microbial fermentation and are used for various purposes, including stabilizing and emulsifying food products. Seaweed-derived agents are extracted from seaweed and are used for their gelling and thickening properties. Animal-derived agents are obtained from animal products and are primarily used for their protein and emulsifying properties. Synthetic agents are chemically synthesized and are used as substitutes for natural texturizing agents in food products.</p></p>
